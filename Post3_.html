HUNT AND KILL ALGORITHM

Ever tried to write a maze generator? Hunt and kill is one of my favourites for maze generation.
 

Foreplay

mazes and mazes everywhere

We all know what a maze is. Bunch of walls , one entrance and one exit, find your way out or rot inside. Mazes are always amazing because they teach you one important lesson - When you run into a dead end , there's nothing to be afraid of turning back and trying a different path because it's always better than just stopping there and staring at that boring wall. 

  • Okay , enough of bullshittery , so how would you generate a maze? One way of doing it is - Take an NxN grid with N*N cells obviously , and put walls on all 4 sides for each cell and let your little piece of code break some random walls , connect some random cells and Viola! You have your maze. 
  • Now let's put that into more of a programmer-ish terms. We have a huge graph where each cell we were just talking about is a node. So generating a maze is basically creating a sub-graph with a path to move between 2 different nodes(start and end). Difficulty of the maze depends on how complicated the path between these 2 particular nodes is. So breaking the wall  between two cells is basically creating a link between two nodes to traverse in-between. 
  • There are an arsenal of algorithms to do that , and hunt and kill is one of them.

The main part!

Hunt and Kill 

In this algorithm there are 2 phases. Hunting and Killing.


start : We start of by selecting a random cell. Then we start the killing phase.

Killing phase : Do a random walk from the current cell - Go to any one of the adjacent cells which are connected to the current one (Horizontally / Vertically ) and kill that cell. (Killing is basically removing the wall between these 2 cells) and repeat this step everytime you kill a cell, making that cell as your current cell. (Remeber that you can only kill someone who's not dead. Don't shoot the corpse ffs i.e don't visit the visited cell again).

Simple isn't it?  But sometimes you run into dead ends where every cell around you is dead or in simple terms all the cells around you are visited and you know the drill - you can't visit a cell which is already visited or when the current cell is surrounded by 3 visited cells and the main wall which is the boundary , so you can't break it. What to do then? You go for hunting, because there's not much left to kill around you so, you set off on a quest to hunt for the next best cell. 

Hunting phase : In the event of a dead end, you start of from the first row in the grid and search cell by cell until you find a cell that satisfies your desires , which are 1) It should be unvisited , 2) It should be connected to a visited cell (Horizontally / Vertically). When you find the cell of your dreams, you break the wall between this cell and the cell it was connected to , then you make this one your current cell and start on a killing spree again. 

end : The algorithm ends when all the cells are dead i.e visited.

Climax

You have a randomly generated maze! Now it's time for you to implement it somewhere.
